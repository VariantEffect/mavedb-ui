Variant Library : Endogenous locus library generation
Variants were generated by introducing all possible single-nucleotide changes at the endogenous BRCA1 locus using CRISPR-Cas9 homology-directed repair with synthetic donor oligonucleotides.
Delivery Method : Lentiviral transduction
Lentiviral vectors were used to deliver guide RNA and repair template constructs into HAP1 cells for CRISPR-based genome editing.
Selection Assay : Cell viability
Cell viability was assessed over an 11-day competitive growth period (~20 cell divisions). Variants that impair BRCA1 function reduce cell fitness, which is measured as depletion of the variant from the cell population relative to synonymous controls. The assay captures both loss-of-function and hypomorphic effects across the RING domain, enabling quantitative classification of missense, nonsense, and splice-site variants.
Organism : Homo sapiens

Abstract

We performed saturation genome editing (SGE) of 13 exons encoding functionally critical domains of BRCA1, generating every possible single-nucleotide variant (SNV) across the RING (exons 2–7) and BRCT (exons 15–23) domains. Variants were introduced into the endogenous BRCA1 locus of HAP1 cells using CRISPR–Cas9-mediated homology-directed repair. Cell viability was measured over 11 days to assess the functional impact of each variant.

This experiment focuses on the RING domain, which is essential for BRCA1's E3 ubiquitin ligase activity. The RING domain mediates the interaction with BARD1, forming a heterodimer crucial for DNA damage repair. Functional scores obtained from this assay provide quantitative measurements of variant impact on protein function, enabling improved variant classification for clinical interpretation.

Method

HAP1 cells were engineered using CRISPR-Cas9 to introduce a library of all possible single-nucleotide variants across exons 2–7 of BRCA1. The sgRNA library was designed to target specific positions within the RING domain, and repair templates containing each variant were supplied for homology-directed repair. Successfully edited cells were selected and expanded over multiple passages.

Cell viability was assessed using a competitive growth assay over 11 days (approximately 20 cell divisions). Variant abundance was measured by deep sequencing at the start (T0) and end (T11) of the culture period. Functional scores were calculated as the log-ratio of variant frequencies normalized to synonymous variants, which were expected to be functionally neutral.

Abstract

We performed saturation genome editing (SGE) of 13 exons encoding functionally critical domains of BRCA1, generating every possible single-nucleotide variant (SNV) across the RING (exons 2–7) and BRCT (exons 15–23) domains. Variants were introduced into the endogenous BRCA1 locus of HAP1 cells using CRISPR–Cas9-mediated homology-directed repair. Cell viability was measured over 11 days to assess the functional impact of each variant.

A total of 3,893 SNVs were scored, representing >96% of all possible single-nucleotide changes in the targeted regions. Functional scores are normalised such that synonymous variants have a mean score of 0 and known pathogenic variants have a mean score of approximately −1.

Methods

HAP1 cells were transduced with lentiviral libraries encoding all possible SNVs in BRCA1 exons 2–7. Editing was performed using SpCas9 with paired guide RNAs flanking each exon. Cells were cultured for 11 days with sampling at days 5, 8 and 11. Variant representation was quantified by amplicon sequencing and functional scores were computed as the log2 ratio of variant frequency at day 11 versus the input library, normalised to synonymous controls.

Abstract

Loss-of-function variants in BRCA1 are associated with increased risk for breast and ovarian cancer. This score set provides functional classification scores for 3,893 single-nucleotide variants in the RING domain, generated through saturation genome editing.

Scores reflect the impact of each variant on cell viability, enabling clinical interpretation of variants of uncertain significance (VUS) in the BRCA1 RING domain.

Methods

Functional scores were computed as the log2 ratio of variant frequency at day 11 endpoint versus input library. Scores were normalized using the median of synonymous variants as the neutral reference and the median of nonsense variants as the loss-of-function reference.

Variants with fewer than 10 reads at input were excluded. Biological replicates (n=3) were combined using inverse-variance weighting.

BRCA1 RING Domain Saturation Genome Editing

urn:mavedb:00000097-a
Cancel

Experiment Information

Title
Short description
Abstract

Loss-of-function variants in BRCA1 are associated with increased risk for breast and ovarian cancer. This study performed saturation genome editing of the RING domain (exons 2–5) to generate comprehensive functional data for clinical variant interpretation.

Using HAP1 haploid cells, we introduced single-nucleotide variants via prime editing and measured their effect on cell viability over 11 days. Functional scores were computed as log2 ratios of variant frequency at endpoint versus input library.

Methods

A saturating SNV library targeting BRCA1 exons 2–5 was generated using prime editing in HAP1 cells. Cells were cultured under standard conditions and harvested at day 0 (input) and day 11 (endpoint).

Genomic DNA was extracted and the target region amplified by PCR. Amplicons were sequenced on an Illumina NovaSeq 6000. Variant calls were made using GATK and functional scores computed as the log2(endpoint frequency / input frequency), normalized to synonymous variant median.
